DON'T FOLLOW THE FOX

Young Willow wore a cloak of green,
The brightest shade you’ve ever seen.

She walked the woods to gather thyme,
And heard a whistle, clear as chime.

“Stay on the path,” her granny pled,
But Willow turned her curious head.

An orange fox with gleaming eyes
Stood still beneath the cloudy skies.
“Come follow me,” the sly fox said,

Through twisting trees where branches spread.
But Willow paused and looked around

She shook her head and stood up tall,
“I think I’ll heed my granny’s call.”

The fox just smirked and slipped from sight,
Back to the woods of mist and night.

She found her thyme and wandered home,
Beneath the sky’s soft twilight dome.
And though the woods still whispered near,
She walked with care, not fear, but cheer.
